Union College Evolutionary Robotics Lab - Tensegrity Team

Objectives

The Evolutionary Robotics Lab at Union College is trying to design complex robots that can adapt behavior to handle dynamic environments.

The Challenges of Controlling Soft Robots

Soft Robotics is a particularly challenging area of interest. Soft Robot = A robot built from soft flexible materials, often with many degrees of freedom.

The properties of Soft Robots make them especially challenging to model and control.

Tensegrities [Tension - Integrity]

Tensegrities are defined by their topology, which is made up of an interconnected network of rigid struts and soft materials to apply tension between struts (ie. springs, cable, rope). The structure uses the force of tension within the soft materials to keep the structure stable.

Current research in the lab is interested in understanding how vibration forces (created by motors) can be used to control robot locomotion. If we can understand how the pre-conditions (initial motor speeds) map to our desired behavior (moving from Point A â†’ Point B) we can successfully control our robot, and eventually use this to accomplish a more complex task. A secondary research goal is finding behaviors that are roost and adaptable, so that even if a robot experiences damage or encounters a new obstacle, it can successfully adapt its behavior to overcome new challenges.
